About Me

Add Photo

I am currently pursuing my PhD in High Energy Physics at Aligarh Muslim University (AMU), Aligarh. My research is centered on theoretical physics, with a specific focus on the formation and decay of Microscopic Black Holes in higher-dimensional space.

My work involves rigorous analysis of Large Hadron Collider (LHC) data to determine exclusion limits and decay signatures. Additionally, I explore interdisciplinary applications of physics principles, such as Information Theory, to biological systems like DNA.

Research Areas

Core Thesis

Microscopic Black Holes

Investigating the theoretical formation of black holes at the TeV scale. My work focuses on Schwarzschild radius calculations in extra dimensions and predicting decay signatures detectable at the LHC.

Interdisciplinary

Biophysics & Information Theory

A comparative study applying statistical mechanics and Shannon entropy to analyze DNA sequences, bridging the gap between theoretical physics and biological complexity.

Publications

Academic Writing

Drafting research papers on the exclusion limits of black hole remnants and their thermodynamic properties. Expertise in LaTeX and scientific documentation.
